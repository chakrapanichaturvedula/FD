Activity · Concept Lock-In
The Rules You Just Discovered

Every answer you gave in the Pen Game was based on one of three rules. Here they are named:

Rule 1 — Upper Bound
Futures price ≤ Spot + Cost of Carry
If futures price exceeds this, anyone can buy spot and short futures for a guaranteed profit. Market forces push it back down.
Rule 2 — Lower Bound
Futures price ≥ Spot + Cost of Carry − Benefits of Holding
If futures price falls below this, anyone can short spot and buy futures for a guaranteed profit. Market forces push it back up.
Rule 3 — Fair Value
Fair Futures = Spot × (1 + rate − dividend_yield) × (DTE/365)
The only price that cannot be arbitraged. When an asset pays income (dividends, convenience yield), futures < spot is completely normal — not bearish.
Contango vs Backwardation in plain terms:

Contango (futures > spot): Normal for equity indices. You pay a financing premium because capital is deferred.

Backwardation (futures < spot): Normal when the asset pays strong income or has high convenience yield. Holding the physical asset is worth more than holding the promise of it.
