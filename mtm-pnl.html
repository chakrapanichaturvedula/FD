Daily MTM logic: Long gains when price rises. Short gains when price falls. The gain or loss is settled that day — not held until final expiry.

Activity 4 · Concept
What does mark-to-market actually mean?
This is what makes futures different from many other instruments. Gains and losses are not held as unrealised positions until expiry — they are actually settled in your margin account every day. This is why the margin balance changes daily.
Mark-to-Market — remember this
Futures P&L is settled daily through mark-to-market — not only at expiry
Daily MTM = (today's settlement − yesterday's settlement) × lot size
Long gains when price rises · Short gains when price falls — every single day
Day 2 compares to Day 1's settlement, not back to the original entry price
