The Two-Choice Comparison — Derive Fair Value
The Setup: You want Nifty 50 exposure for the next 30 days. You have exactly ₹22,00,000 available. You have two equally valid ways to get this exposure. Confirm each step to build the fair price.
Fair Value Building Up
Fair Value = ₹22,000 + ??? − ???
1
Start: Nifty Spot Today
₹22,000
Both choices start from the same underlying: the Nifty 50 index at ₹22,000 per unit. One lot = 50 units = ₹11,00,000 contract value at this price.
A Buy spot now — Pay ₹22,00,000. Own the stocks. Receive dividends. Capital is fully deployed.
B Buy futures — Post margin (~₹1,10,000). The remaining ₹20,90,000 stays in a T-bill earning interest.
2
Add: Financing Cost (the futures buyer earns this)
+ ???
The futures buyer keeps their ₹22,00,000 in T-bills for 30 days. The spot buyer tied up all their capital and earns nothing on it. This interest advantage must be priced in — futures should cost more than spot to offset the interest the futures buyer earns.
Risk-free rate (91-day T-bill)6.50% / year
Days to expiry30 days
Daily rate (6.5% ÷ 365)0.01781% / day
Interest on ₹22,00,000 for 30 days₹22,00,000 × 6.5% × 30/365
Financing cost per unit+ ₹117.53
Running fair value so far: ₹22,000 + ₹117.53 = ₹22,117.53
3
Subtract: Dividends Foregone (spot holder earns, futures holder doesn't)
− ???
The spot buyer receives Nifty dividends during the 30 days. The futures buyer gets nothing until expiry. This dividend advantage of holding spot means futures should cost less — subtract the dividend income from the fair price.
Nifty dividend yield (estimated)1.20% / year
Dividend income for 30 days₹22,000 × 1.2% × 30/365
Dividend adjustment per unit− ₹21.70
Running fair value: ₹22,117.53 − ₹21.70 = ₹22,095.83
✓
Fair Futures Price
₹22,095.83
The Formula You Just Built
Fair Value = S × [1 + (r − d) × T/365]
= 22,000 × [1 + (0.065 − 0.012) × 30/365]
= 22,000 × [1 + 0.053 × 0.0822]
= ₹22,095.83
Where: S = spot price, r = risk-free rate, d = dividend yield, T = days to expiry
