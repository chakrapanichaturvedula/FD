Activity 1 · Spot or Not
Identify the spot transaction
A spot transaction means trade and settlement happen now or near-immediately. Which of these is a spot transaction?
Spot markets are where you buy or sell today and receive or deliver the asset immediately (or within a standard short settlement window like T+1). All other choices involve an agreement today but exchange at a later date — that is the world of forwards, futures, and options.

Activity 3 · Positions
Long or short — and who gains?
Trader A buys one Nifty futures contract expecting the market to rise.
Trader B sells one Nifty futures contract expecting the market to fall.

The next day Nifty futures settle +200 points higher. Who gains and who loses?
Long = benefits when price rises. Short = benefits when price falls.

This is direction language, not time language. Long does not mean "held for a long time" and short does not mean "small position." These words describe which side of the market you are on.
4
Activity 4 · Closing a Position
What does squaring off mean?
Priya bought 100 Nifty futures contracts (long). She wants to close her position before expiry. What should she do?
Squaring off means taking the opposite transaction in the same contract and quantity, bringing your net position to zero.

Long 100 + Sell 100 = Net 0. No open position means no further price risk from that trade. You can square off at any time before expiry — you do not have to wait.

Practice · Calculation
Calculate a short futures P&L
Priya shorts one Nifty futures contract. Calculate her total profit or loss.
Entry price
21,800
Closing price
21,600
Lot size
50
Short P&L = (Entry − Exit) × Lot size
= (21,800 − 21,600) × 50
= 200 × 50 = ₹10,000 profit
