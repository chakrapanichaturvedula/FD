Hedger Path · Priya's Scenarios
Hedger's Nightmare — What Basis Risk Actually Looks Like
Priya's hedge: She shorts 1 Nifty lot (50 units) of futures at ₹22,380. Her portfolio value = ₹11,00,000 (50 units × ₹22,000). Run each scenario below to see what basis risk looks like in practice.
Key insight across all scenarios: Priya eliminated directional market risk — but the basis is still uncertain. When she must close her hedge before expiry, the basis at that moment determines her actual outcome. This unremovable uncertainty is called basis risk.

⚡ Twist: What if transaction costs are ₹500/leg?
Total transaction costs = ₹500 × 2 = ₹1,000. Your locked profit before costs was ₹9,914. After costs: ₹9,914 − ₹1,000 = ₹8,914. Still profitable.

Break-even question: What is the minimum mispricing (in ₹/unit) needed to cover ₹1,000 in costs?
₹1,000 ÷ 50 units = ₹20/unit. The current mispricing is ₹198. You need at least ₹20 to break even — this trade clears that easily.

Synthesis · Both Paths
Two Lenses, One Number

The same basis of −380 (vs fair −182) means something completely different to each participant. Here's the full comparison:

Priya — Hedger 🛡️
Basis is…Residual risk after hedging
She wants…Basis to be stable & predictable
Deviation hurts when…She must close before expiry & basis moved against her
Fair basis means…Clean hedge — outcome matches expectation
Mihir — Arbitrageur ⚡
Basis is…A price signal — mispricing = locked profit
He wants…Basis to deviate from fair — then converge
Deviation hurts when…It corrects before he can trade it
Fair basis means…No arb opportunity — market is efficient
